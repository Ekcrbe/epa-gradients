Methodology

The metric — percentile displacement

With F_global the worldwide CDF of team strength and Q_region the region’s quantile function, on a within-region percentile axis q (a team’s standing among its region’s teams):

D(q) = F_global( Q_region(q) ) − q

D(q) > 0 means a team at the q-th percentile of its region ranks higher than that worldwide — the region is locally harder (deeper). D(q) < 0 is locally easier. Where the curve crosses zero, a region flips from easier to harder (or vice versa) — the shape of each curve is left for the plot itself rather than reduced to a single number. A worldwide-percentile variant, p − F_region(Q_global(p)), is retained in the data.

Team strength

Each snapshot is a postseason (2008–2026): the competitive state after that season. A region’s teams are those that competed in that season — so the count reflects the region’s current depth, not long-defunct teams. Strength is Statbotics’ unitless EPA (an Elo-style, cross-season-comparable rating, ~1500 average). By default each team’s strength is a linear-recency weighted moving average (weights 4:3:2:1) of its unitless EPA over the four most recent seasons up to and including that one, which smooths a living team’s rating; the Single-year EPA toggle uses that season’s EPA alone. The canceled 2020 and 2021 seasons are dropped from every window (and produce no snapshot).

Regions

Present-day boundaries are applied to every season back to 2008. Districts are mapped by team state (multi-state districts such as New England, Chesapeake, and Mid-Atlantic included). Two exceptions are encoded explicitly: South Carolina (its own region through 2022, Peachtree in 2023–24, FIRST South Carolina from 2025) and Pennsylvania (split between Mid-Atlantic and non-district). Non-district teams are grouped by US state, with Québec and Ontario as their own regions and other countries grouped by country — except for a few small states combined with their neighbors into more competitively coherent regions: Upper Midwest (Minnesota, North Dakota, South Dakota) and WOW (non-district Pennsylvania, Ohio, West Virginia).

Baseline & uncertainty

The worldwide baseline is the distribution of all teams for that season (or pooled across all seasons), including the region itself. The shaded band is a 95% bootstrap confidence interval (the region resampled against a fixed world). Small regions are noisy: those with fewer than five teams show no band, and n is shown everywhere — read small samples with the band and count in mind.

Depth ratio & average difficulty

R(x) = (1 − F_region(x)) / (1 − F_global(x)) compares a region’s share of teams above each skill level to the world’s, on a log axis over the global 1st–99th percentile (the 100th is skipped to avoid dividing by zero). Above 1 = deeper than average at that level. The plain mean of R across that same range is each region’s single average difficulty number — the most meaningful one-number summary, and what sorts the All Regions heatmap and small multiples below.
